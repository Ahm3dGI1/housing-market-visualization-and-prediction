Market Trends
Trend 1
Our line graph provides a historical analysis of the sale price per square foot in various San Francisco neighborhoods. It shows significant fluctuations in areas like Pacific Heights, indicative of a volatile real estate market. Conversely, neighborhoods like the Inner Sunset exhibit more stability. This data is crucial for understanding long-term price trends and could guide potential investments and housing policies.
Trend 2
This forecast graph employs the ARIMA model to predict the future sale prices per square foot, based on historical trends. The primary trend line, accompanied by a shaded confidence interval, suggests a continuing increase in property prices. This forecast is vital for understanding future market directions and could be used by stakeholders to inform strategic planning and decision-making.
Trend 3
Through this parallel coordinates plot, a multi-variable comparison is drawn between neighborhoods, depicting average house values alongside the number of housing units and gross rent. The graph is a complex representation that could assist in revealing patterns for city planners and analysts, highlighting disparities and potential areas of housing market stress.
Trend 4
This bar chart methodically ranks the most expensive neighborhoods in San Francisco by average price per square foot. It is evident from the chart that the Union Square District is currently the most expensive area. This is a valuable indicator for real estate investors and buyers looking for property in premium locations and serves as a benchmark for the high-end market in the city.
Trend 5
The heatmap illustrates the distribution of gross rent across San Francisco, using a color gradient to denote the intensity of rent levels. Neighborhoods with higher rents are depicted in darker shades, which could be valuable for potential tenants seeking affordable housing and for policymakers focused on housing affordability issues.
About Us
 Our approach to tackling the housing challenges in the Bay Area revolves around providing a clear and detailed view of the housing market to everyone. Recognizing the pressing issue of homelessness and the increasing burden of rent on residents, we focused on developing a solution that empowers individuals with the knowledge to make informed decisions about their housing options.
